Welcome to 4th NATIONAL RESEARCH CONFERENCE

The Philippine Association of Practitioners of Student Affairs and Services (PAPSAS, Inc.), in partnership with PAPSAS Region V and Bicol University, is excited to announce its upcoming 4th National Research Conference.

Mark Your Calendars:

When: June 18-20, 2025

Where: Proxy by The Oriental Albay, Purok 1 Brgy. 33 Penaranda St, Legazpi Port District, Legazpi City, Albay

Theme: “Champions for Change: Leveraging Research for SAS Practitioners”

Thus, we invite you and your SAS practitioners to present their paper on any of the following sub-themes:

  1. SAS Practitioners: Creating Pathways Toward Inclusion, Diversity, and Equality
    This sub-theme includes presentations on marginalized students, gender equality, scholarships, international students, and cultural diversity on campus.
  2. Technology in Quality Student Campus Life: Promises and Challenges
    This sub-theme includes presentations on using digital platforms and innovations in SAS practice, including artificial intelligence (AI).
  3. Campus Engagement and Practices in Nurturing a Healthy Campus Environment
    This sub-theme includes papers on student wellbeing, SAS wellbeing, the mental health of the academic community, safe space, student– caring, and student-friendly campus.
  4. All Roads Lead to Sustainable Development Goals
    This sub-theme includes student-initiated and OSA-initiated activities, voluntarism, and community engagement toward sustainable development goals.
  5. The Era of the Cyber Space and Student Values Development
    This sub-theme includes papers on the responsible use of social media platforms, discipline and formation practices, and student violations in cyberspace.

For those who are interested in presenting a paper, the deadline for the submission of a 300–350-word abstract with not more than 5 keywords is May 15, 2025. Please include the abstract's title, rationale, objectives, significance, scope, research design, and findings. Submission of the whole paper is June 10, 2025.

Mechanics and Guidelines for Paper Presentation

Qualifications

  1. All interested SAS practitioners, scholars, students and researchers are qualified to submit paper for presentation.
  2. The research must be related to any of the following sub-themes:
    • SAS Practitioners: Creating Pathways Toward Inclusion, Diversity, and Equality
    • Technology in Quality Student Campus Life: Promises and Challenges
    • Campus Engagement and Practices in Nurturing a Healthy Campus Environment
    • All Roads Lead to Sustainable Development Goals
    • The Era of the Cyber Space and Student Values Development

Submission and Selection

  1. Interested presenters should submit the abstract of their paper using this link: https://bit.ly/4thResearchConference
  2. The abstract should have a maximum of three hundred fifty (350) words, and it must be written in English in MS Word Times New Roman 12 format, typed single-spaced in A4 size with one inch margin on all sides, and must not exceed one page. (See attached sample).
  3. The PAPSAS In. Research Committee will hold a screening of the applications based on the submitted abstract.
  4. Authors of selected papers for presentation shall submit the full paper until June 10, 2025.

Full Paper

  1. The full paper should follow the IMRAD format which is subdivided into the following sections:
    • title
    • authors and institutional affiliations
    • abstract
    • introduction
    • methodology
    • results and discussion
    • conclusion and recommendations
    • reference list/literature cited
    • appendices (optional)
  2. The full paper should be written in MS Word Times New Roman 12 format, typed single-spaced (zero spacing before and after paragraph) in A4 size with one inch margin on all sides, and must not exceed should not be more than 15 pages excluding appendices and no more than 5000 words.
  3. Main section headings should be in bold face, UPPERCASE, and aligned on the left.
  4. Subheadings should be sentence-cased, bold case, italicized, and aligned on the left.
  5. Page number should be on the upper right corner of the page.
  6. Do not separate pages from the main sections.
  7. The title should be at the top of the first page, 14 points, centered, single spaced, and in UPPERCASE.
  8. The authors should be in sentence case, 12 points, single spaced, centered, and with superscript number for institutional affiliations placed after the authors’ names.
  9. The institutional affiliations and address of authors should be in sentence case, 11 points, single spaced, centered, and superscript number placed before the institutional affiliations and address.
  10. The abstract should have a maximum of three hundred fifty (350) words in 11 points font size. Below the abstract after the blank line, are the three to five (3 to 5) keywords with indention. The “Keywords:” label should be italicized while the actual keywords are not italicized and written in lowercase letters each is separated by commas.
  11. Figures and tables should follow the APA 7 style.
  12. Only the citations used in the paper should be included in the reference list. References should be alphabetically arranged. (See attached sample).
